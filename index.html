Why Web Development?

I have spent my entire adult life working with technology. My strengths include an inane ability to quickly grasp its concepts, understand the many nuances, to articulate technical topics to non-technical audiences, and to formulate solutions. These skills have proven to be very useful and have afforded me opportunities to explore numerous roles. From operations to support, mainframe to cloud, application administration to system design, I have amassed a catalog of experience and broad base of knowledge across many technical disciplines.

With such a variety of experiences, I have developed the requisite skills and the reputation of a capable IT generalist. While the name sounds humble, individuals like this are essential to an organization. It is often an IT generalist who bridges the gap between the deep subject matter expertise and the tactile needs of project or initiative. An IT generalist can reach across knowledge silos to build and manage a coalition of talent.

The challenge is there is no template for IT generalists. There's no degree, certification, or standardized test to identify these people. It is difficult to advertise and actively pursue a set of nearly intangible traits which enable someone to succeed while serving in this capacity. As as result, almost nobody recruits for what they can't adequately define.

After nearly three decades in IT, I felt it was time to develop depth in something. I wanted a a demonstrable specialty which would help qualify my experience. It is that which prompted me to explore web development.
